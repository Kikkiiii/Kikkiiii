What are Great White Sharks?

The great white shark is a species of large mackerel shark that can be found on the surface waters of all major oceans.

They are well known for their large sizing between 4.6 and 6 meters long.

The great white shark is known to have a variety of prays including fish, other sharks and sea birds.

They have a torpedo-shaped body allowing them to cruise in waters really well and be able to speed up when hunting.

They have an acute sense of smell and can sense electric fields through their snout.

Where do Great white sharks live?

Great white sharks are found in mostly temperate waters but are more commonly found around Auith Australia, Africa and Northern California.

Impacts of humanity

Over ⅓ of all sharks are being endangered due to overfishing.

They have also become rare due to years of hunting by man wanting their fins and teeth.

They are often caught as bycatch by commercial fisheries and can become entangled in meshes.

This beautiful animal is only endanger because of human activity, being at the top of the food chain human kind is the only species that is causing it extreme harm.
