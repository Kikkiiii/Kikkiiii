Sea Otters

What are Sea Otters?

Sea Otters are a marine mammal, they typically weign between 14 and 45kg.

The sea otter main form of insulation is their very thick coat making it the densest in the animal kingdom.

They are capable fo walking on land or living exclusively in the ocean.

Their main source of diet comes from sea urchins, mollusks and crustaceans and some species of fish.

They dive down to catch these fish and use tools like rocks to open shells of its prey.

Where do sea otters live?

Sea otters live in kelp forest and estuary habitats, they are a very important part for these areas since their good health indicates that the area is also in good health.

Impacts of humanity

Because of the rise in tempretures due to global warming, Otters are finding it harder to hunt for their pray since the warm waters is driving them away.

The increase of carbon dioxide has lead ocean to acidification

Meaning that the shell fish life, sea otters main source of food is declining quickly, meaning there being no food for these adorabe otters.

Sea otters are a key stone animal, if they perish then hundreads of different enviourments will suffer.
