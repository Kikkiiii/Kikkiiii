Hammer Head Shark

What are Hammer Head Sharks?

Hammer head sharks form the family Sphyrnidae, they are named for their unusual form of their head. Their head is flattened and laterally extended resembling the shape of a hammer.

Their evolution may explain the shape of their head, spacing their eyes further away gives them a 360 view.

This gives them an above and under view at all times. Their head shape also helped them with balance and manoverty, preventing them from loosing balance.

This unique evolution of their head is also related to their sensory function, by having their head more spread out it allows for these sensory pores to receive information from more ground.

Where do hammer head sharks live?

They are found to live in tropical and subtropical coastal waters, they are found throughout the seas around northern Australia. This also includes the Atliantic Ocean and has been found in North Carolina etc.

Impacts of humanity

There are two distinct populations that are listed as endangered.

As many other sharks they are hunted and wanted for their fin trade.

They are mostly hunted for in the Northern Terriroty and Western Australia until the Queensland government proposed a protection act for all hammerhead species in 2024.Altho this didn’t fix the issue of the hunting since they are still caught in fishing nets in the Great Barrier Reef.


Fun Facts:

  1. There are nine species of hammerhead sharks in the world.
  2. Their distinctive ‘hammer-head’ part is called cephalofoil.
  3. Hammerhead sharks can tan.
  4. Hammerheads love to travel in groups.
  5. They are not as old as other shark species.
